Hadrius Support Structure Alignment
A Tactical Alignment Guide for Account Managers & Support Scaling
Last Updated: July 16, 2026

________________

Purpose

Support is now taking ownership of triaging the majority of incoming tickets, a responsibility that has historically fallen to Account Managers and CSMs alongside their strategic relationship work. Going forward, only tickets identified as belonging to the AM/CSM team, such as advisory, billing, contracts, and high-tier onboarding requests, will be routed directly to AM/CSM, bypassing Support entirely.

For the AM/CSM team, this means the day-to-day burden of fielding transactional and administrative tickets goes away. AMs and CSMs will only see the tickets that genuinely require their advisory input or relationship ownership, freeing them to focus on strategic account work instead of tactical support volume. This document lays out how that split works in practice.

1. Support Owns Triage

Support (Stephen & Jacob) will handle the majority of incoming tickets, removing that load from AMs.

2. Automate & Deflect

The goal is to automate and deflect to self-service wherever possible, reducing overall incoming ticket volume rather than just processing it faster. As part of this, Support will focus on improving the existing FAQs, Knowledge Base articles, and videos so more issues can be self-served before a ticket is opened.

3. Auto-Routing for AM-Only Tickets

Tickets that clearly belong to the AM team (advisory compliance interpretation, billing/pricing/contracts, high-tier onboarding strategy, etc.) will auto-route directly to the AM queue or individual, bypassing Support triage entirely.

Note: We'll need to work with the AMs directly to identify the full set of ticket types that should always be routed to them, so the auto-routing rules reflect what they actually consider AM-only work.

4. SLAs

SLAs follow the structure below:

Issue Type | Target Initial Response | Follow Up SLA | Target Resolution | Breach Warning
---|---|---|---|---
High Priority / P0 | < 1 Hour | < 30 mins | < 4 Hours | Triggers at 1 Hour
Standard / P1 | < 2 Hours | < 2 Hours | < 24 Hours | Triggers at 2 Hours
Procedural / P2 | < 4 Hours | < 2 Hours | < 48 Hours | Triggers at 4 Hours

5. Continuous Automation

Support will use several methods to continuously add new automated workflows as bottlenecks are identified, rather than treating automation as a one-time setup.

6. Escalation to AM

When Support can't resolve a ticket without AM/CS input, it escalates following this path:

1. Tag & Assign: Support changes the ticket owner in Pylon to the designated AM.
2. Internal Context Handoff: Support adds an internal note tagging the AM with the problem, what's already been investigated, and the suggested action needed.
3. Handoff Notification: The AM gets a native Pylon notification and a Slack alert; ticket status changes to "Escalated" so it's easily filtered.
